White Terror of the Qinghai Rhododendrons

Qinghai Province · Summer 2017

Had it not been for the white rhododendrons blanketing the mountainside, the snow lingering on the peaks of the Qilian Range, and the crystalline air of four thousand meters, I might have left with the same emptiness carved into the riverbanks and hollowed out of the mountains themselves.

Policies descended from above.

Cigarette butts accumulated below.

I walked across a mound of illegally excavated gravel, measuring distances that would later become estimates in a damage assessment report.

A local official waited nearby.

Small in stature, weathered by the plateau, and speaking with a thick local accent, he never stopped smoking.

One cigarette after another.

We were standing more than four thousand meters above sea level.

I could not help wondering how his lungs endured what the altitude had already made scarce.

In recent years, environmental policies had arrived one after another.

Ecological redlines.

Ecological compensation.

Green GDP.

Each new directive burned brightly for a day, much like the cigarette between his fingers.

Then the smoke drifted away.

The consequences, however, remained for a lifetime.
We climbed higher into the valley.

Snow still rested on the ridges above us.

The shaded slopes were covered by carpets of Qinghai rhododendrons in full bloom.

Their pale blossoms seemed to exist for beauty alone.

Then, around the bend, another quarry appeared.

An entire mountainside had been sliced away.

Standing there, I felt as though something had been cut from me as well.
